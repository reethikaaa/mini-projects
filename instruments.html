Oval sistrum rattle

The Sistrum is a percussion instrument that originated in Ancient Egypt. It was shaken during religious ceremonies and when coming into the presence of a deity. Presently it is used during village festivals. Each is one of a kind and will vary in size and shape.

African balafon 5keys

The balafon is a true work of art and an integral member of the idiophone family of tuned percussion instruments which includes the xylophone, marimba, glockenspiel, and vibraphone. The balafon's beautiful sound is produced by striking the tuned keys with two padded sticks.

Ocarina

An ocarina is a type of wind instrument, or aerophone, in which sound is produced by the vibration of air. Unlike flutes, ocarinas are not tubes but consist of one or more enclosed, rounded chambers. They produce sound when air vibrates within the enclosure and exits through a hle near the mouthpiece.

African string instrument

The Kora of Africa is a unique aspect to a harp and a notched bridge like a lute or guitar. The first known reference to the Kora from Mungo Park in 1799 in his book "Journey into Africa Districts Interns." He describes as "a large harp with 18 strings."
